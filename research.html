Cancer Biology West Africa GWAS Open Access
BRCA1/2 Mutation Landscape in Sub-Saharan African Women: A Pan-African Genome-Wide Association Study of 48,200 Participants
Osei A., Mensah K., Nwosu E., Abiola R., et al. (22 authors) · Nature Genetics
Whole-genome sequencing across 14 African nations identifies 38 novel BRCA1/2 variants absent from European-derived reference panels, with allele frequencies differing significantly from non-African populations. Findings directly challenge the adequacy of current clinical risk-scoring tools when applied to African patients and call for ancestry-specific screening protocols. Population-attributable risk calculations suggest 23% of hereditary breast cancers in West Africa are currently misclassified using existing guidelines.

Cross-Ancestry Alzheimer's Risk Prediction Using Multi-Modal Machine Learning: ApoE4 Stratification Across 12,000 Diverse Patients
Chen R., Dlamini N., Okafor J., Williams T., et al. · Cell
A transformer-based multi-modal model integrating genetic variants, clinical biomarkers, and neuroimaging achieves 84.3% AUC for Alzheimer's onset prediction across ethnically diverse cohorts — outperforming single-ancestry baseline models by 11.2 percentage points. The model demonstrates particular improvement in African-American and Ghanaian participants, subgroups historically underperformed by existing clinical tools. Code and model weights are fully open-sourced.

FedHealth: Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning for Cancer Prediction Across 14 African Hospitals Without Raw Data Sharing
Dlamini N., Mensah K., Okafor J., et al. · Nature Medicine
A federated learning framework enables 14 hospitals across 8 African countries to collaboratively train a cancer risk model without centralizing patient data. The federated model achieves 88.7% accuracy — within 2.3% of a hypothetical centralized model — while maintaining full data sovereignty at each site.
