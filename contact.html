FAQ

Common
Questions

Answers to the most frequent questions we receive from researchers, institutions, and collaborators around the world.

How do I submit a dataset or publication to GenomAtlas? +
Submit via our Community page or email research@genomAtlas.org. All submissions require a brief description, geographic scope, disease area, and ethical approval documentation (for human subject data). We aim to confirm receipt within 48 hours and provide full peer feedback within 14 days.
Is GenomAtlas data free to use for research? +
Yes — all data on GenomAtlas is open access for non-commercial research purposes. Datasets are published under Creative Commons licenses (typically CC-BY-4.0) that require attribution. Commercial use requires a separate agreement with the originating institution. We never sell data ourselves.
How do I get API access? +
Email api@genomAtlas.org with a brief description of your use case, institution, and intended query volume. We provide authenticated API keys for researchers and developers. Rate limits vary by tier. Full API documentation is available in our Developer Portal.
Can I use GenomAtlas AI tools in my own research pipeline? +
All GenomAtlas AI tools (CancerSeq AI, NeuroRisk Engine, PathVision, etc.) are fully open-source and available on GitHub under MIT licence. Model weights and training code are included. We encourage integration and ask only for attribution in publications.
How do I propose an institutional partnership? +
Use the contact form above with the topic "Research Collab", or email hello@genomAtlas.org directly. Describe your institution, research focus, and proposed collaboration scope. Our partnerships team will respond within 5 business days to schedule an exploratory call.
What ethical standards must submitted data meet? +
All human subject data must be accompanied by documented ethical approval from a recognised IRB, REC, or national equivalent. Data must be de-identified per HIPAA or equivalent standards. We follow FAIR data principles and indigenous data sovereignty frameworks for community-owned datasets. Full ethics guidelines are available in our Data Policy.
