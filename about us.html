About GenomAtlas

Science Without
Borders.
Data Without Bias.

GenomAtlas was founded on a simple but urgent conviction: the world's most critical health data belongs to everyone — especially to the communities most burdened by disease. We are a global, open-access scientific platform bridging research gaps between the US, Africa, and the rest of the world.

Our Mission

Closing the Global
Health Data Gap

African populations account for over 17% of the world's population but represent less than 3% of published genomic research. The consequences are devastating — drugs, diagnostics, and risk models built on biased data fail the people who need them most.

GenomAtlas exists to fix that. We aggregate, curate, and democratize health data — from cancer genomics to Alzheimer's biomarkers — with a dedicated focus on US populations and the 54 nations of Africa. We build AI tools designed to work in low-resource settings. And we connect researchers across continents to collaborate, share, and get feedback.

This is not charity. This is science done right.

01

Equity First

Every decision — from dataset curation to AI model training — is evaluated for its equity implications. Underrepresented populations are not an afterthought; they are our priority.

02

Open by Default

All data, tools, and publications on GenomAtlas are open access wherever legally and ethically possible. Science progresses fastest when knowledge is shared freely.

03

Rigorously Scientific

Peer review, transparent methodology, and reproducibility are non-negotiable. We hold every submission — including our own — to the highest scientific standards.

04

Community Powered

GenomAtlas grows through its contributors. Researchers, clinicians, and data scientists from Lagos to Los Angeles shape what we build and what we prioritize.

Our Story

How We Got Here

2019

The Idea Forms

Frustrated by the near-total absence of African genomic data in landmark cancer studies, our founders — computational biologists working between Accra and Baltimore — begin assembling a private research consortium. The question: what would a truly global cancer database look like?

2020

First Datasets, First Partnerships

The consortium digitizes and anonymizes 8,000 breast cancer records from Nigerian teaching hospitals — the first step toward a pan-African oncology repository. Partnership agreements signed with three US research universities and two African academic medical centers.

2021

Platform Launch & AI Division

GenomAtlas launches publicly with 1,200 curated datasets. The AI & Computational Biology division is established, releasing the first version of CancerSeq AI — trained on multi-ethnic genomic data. First preprint published with 14 co-authors across 6 countries.

2022

Alzheimer's Research Program Begins

Growing evidence that Alzheimer's biomarkers and risk factors differ substantially across ancestries prompts the launch of a dedicated neurodegeneration research program. NeuroRisk Engine v1.0 released. Collaboration established with US-based Alzheimer's research centers.

2023

Federated Learning & Rural Deployment

FedHealth framework enables 14 African hospitals to collaboratively train AI models without sharing raw patient data. PathVision deployed in 6 rural East African clinics for cervical cancer screening. Repository crosses 10,000 dataset milestone.

2024–25

Today: 14,800+ Datasets, 62 Countries

GenomAtlas is now the largest open-access repository of health data focused on African and underrepresented global populations. With 940 active contributors, 6 AI tools, and a growing community of researchers, we are just getting started.

Ethics & Data Governance

Data Sovereignty
is Non-Negotiable

We believe that the people whose data is collected must always benefit from its use. GenomAtlas enforces strict data sovereignty principles: all datasets retain full attribution to their source institution and community. No data is commercialized without explicit consent.

All human subject data submissions require documented ethical approval from recognized IRBs or RECs. We follow FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) principles for all data, and are committed to indigenous data sovereignty frameworks for African community data.

🔐
Ethical Review Required

All human subject datasets require documented IRB or REC approval before submission is accepted.

⚖️
FAIR Data Principles

Every dataset on GenomAtlas must meet Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, and Reusable standards.

🌍
Community Attribution

Source communities and institutions are always credited. Data cannot be republished or commercialized without explicit permission.

🚫
No Data Exploitation

GenomAtlas will never sell, license for profit, or share data with commercial entities without documented community consent.

🔒
Anonymization Standards

All individual-level data is de-identified per HIPAA and international equivalents before publication on the platform.
